Understanding Drug Interaction

Sometimes one medicine can interact adversely with another medicine in your body, and this can change how strongly the medicines work or whether they have side effects. This is called an interaction, and it can also happen when medicines interact with certain foods or drinks (including alcohol).



Do you know most drug-induced deaths are in the context of poly-drug use?


Protect yourself by avoiding the combinations of these commonly used drugs

Valium Interaction


Dont Mix

  • Opioid such as oxycodone, hydromorphone, heroin
  • Tranquilizers such as alprazolam and lorazepam
  • Sleep medication such as zolpidem
  • Methamphetamine


Overdose Risk

  • Loss of consciousness
  • Addiction
  • Accidents
  • Coma
  • Death




Codeine Interaction


Dont Mix

  • Benzodiazepines such as Valium
  • Opioid analgesics/Naltrexone
  • CYP2D6 substrates/Panobinostat
  • Tramadol/Sodium Oxybate


Overdose Risk

  • Decreased heart rate
  • Mental numbness
  • Accidents
  • Coma
  • Death




Prozac Interaction


Dont Mix

  • Benzodiazepines
  • Tricyclic antidepressants (TCAs)
  • Monoamine oxidase inhibitors (MAOIs) such as Pimozide and Thioridazine
  • CYP2D6 substrates


Overdose Risk

  • Increased risk of suicidal thoughts
  • Insomnia
  • Vasodilatation
  • Sexual dysfunction
  • Death




Morphine Interaction


Dont Mix

  • Opioids such as Codeine
  • Sleep medication such as Zolpidem
  • Antihistamines such as Cetirizine, Diphenhydramine
  • Muscle relaxants such as Carisoprodol, Cyclobenzaprine


Overdose Risk

  • Trouble breathing
  • Slow heartbeat
  • Coma
  • Death

  • Pexidartinib
  • Prilocaine
  • Sodium nitrite
  • Teriflunomide

  • Pain in upper stomach
  • Dark urine

Oxycodone Interaction


Dont Mix

  • Antidepressant
  • Opioids such as codeine
  • Sleep medication such as Zolpidem
  • Muscle relaxants such as carisoprodol, cyclobenzaprine


Overdose Risk

  • Loss of consciousness
  • Chest pain
  • Slow heart-beat
  • Coma
  • Death


Tips to Minimize the Risk of Interaction

  • Be honest with your doctor and share all prescriptions and medical history details.
  • Avoid sharing medications as everyone's medical needs are diffrent.
  • Read labels on the medications for any dosage, warnings etc.
  • Check manufacturing date and expiry date before consuming any medications.
  • When in doubt, check the nearby pharmacy or seek medical help.
