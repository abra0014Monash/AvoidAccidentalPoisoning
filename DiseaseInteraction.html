Understanding Disease Interaction

A disease interaction happens when a drug which is intented to use for therapeutic purpose causes harful effect on the patient's body due to interaction with the disease or patient's condition.Drug- disease interaction can happen for both prescribed and over the counter drugs.It is often recommended to consult with the doctor if a patient has a disease and is unsure about drug interaction as care must be taken to choose therapeutic drugs.

Consult the doctor if you consume listed drugs during Coronary Heart Disease

  • Non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDS).
  • Cold and cough medicines with pseudoephedrine or phenylephrine.
  • Alka-Seltzer®
  • Calcium channel blockers such as diltiazem (Cardizem) or verapamil (Calan, Verelan).

  • Diclofenac (Voltaren, Cambia)
  • Amiodarone (Cordarone, Pacerone)
  • Allopurinol (Zyloprim)
  • Anti-seizure medications
  • Risperidone (Risperdal) and quetiapine (Seroquel)
