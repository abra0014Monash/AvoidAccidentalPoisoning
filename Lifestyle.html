Lifestyle and Medicine

Do you know that some medicine could have adverse interactions with food consumption and physical exercise?

There could also be side effects caused by the interactions between food-medicine and exercise-medicine with unpredictable consequences.

Food and Medicine Interaction

Some food may change the medicine effects and cause harm to the user 's body.

Below are some dangerous combinations between food and medicine.



Certain food with high amount of vitamin K can reduce the effectiveness of warfarin medicine. There are other critical risks listed below:

  • Increase the possibility of stroke
  • Increase the possibility of heart attack

Examples of high vitamin K food:

  • Kale
  • Spinach
  • Turnips
  • Brussels sprouts


Tyramine-containing foods & monoamine oxidase is a bad combination with the following risks:

  • Hypertensive crises
  • Dangerous increases of blood pressure

Examples of tyramine-containing foods:

  • Red wine
  • Malt beer
  • Aged cheese
  • Dried fruits


Antibiotics like tetracycline, doxycycline, and ciprofloxacin may be affected by dairy products with the following risks:

  • Antibiotics may bind to calcium in milk, forming an insoluble substance in the stomach and upper small intestine that the body cannot absorb.

Examples of calcium-rich foods:

  • Milk
  • Yogurt
  • Cheese


Potential risks of grapefruit & statins:

  • Muscle soreness
  • Liver abnormalities

Potential risks of grapefruit & calcium channel blockers:

  • High blood pressure

Potential risks of grapefruit & erectile dysfunction drugs:

  • Headaches with fatal or near-fatal risks
  • Flushing
  • Low blood pressure

Exercise and Medicine Interaction

The physiological changes caused by exercise may change the absorption, distribution, metabolism and elimination of certain drugs for common diseases.

The following list shows the types of medications you should be aware of when doing physical exercise.

Medicine Type Possible Consequences of Medicine and Exercise Interaction
Antidepressant Extreme fatigue, low motivation, increases the likelihood of self-harm.
Sleeping Pill Cause hangovers during the day, make you feel groggy and drowsy, increase the likelihood of self-harm.
Diabetes Medication Lower blood sugar to a dangerous level.
Fluoroquinolone Antibiotics Lead to painful inflammation and/or tendon tears, increase the likelihood of tendon rupture.
Oral Decongestants Raise heart rate or blood pressure, make you feel tired and may cause injury during exercise faster than usual, increase risk of heart disease or arrhythmias like irregular heartbeats.
Opioid Pain Relievers and Muscle Relaxants Prevent your nerves from telling your brain when you are in pain, increase the likelihood of self-harm.
Anabolic Steroids Overuse of these drugs to support exercise may lead to: cardiovascular disease, sudden cardiac death, myocardial infarction, liver problems, tumors and other types of injury, tendon rupture, osteoporosis, bone loss.
