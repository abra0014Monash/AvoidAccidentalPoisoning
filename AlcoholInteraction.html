Understanding Alcohol Interaction

Alcohol consumption together with medication use can cause adverse effects on the body such as nausea, vomiting, headaches, fainting or drowsiness. Reconsider consuming alcohol and medication together if you are unsure about the interactions of particular drugs with alcohol. Learn more about a medicine and whether it will interact with alcohol. Also talk to pharmacists or health care providers.


Alchohol Interaction with Disease



If you have IHD issues and consume alcohol, the cardiovascular system may be affected and cause the following risks:

  • Increased heart rate
  • Increased blood pressure
  • Weakened heart muscle
  • Arrhythmia
  • Atrial fibrillation
  • Intermittent and chronic alcoholism


If you have diabetes, drinking alcohol may cause your blood sugar to either rise or fall and even cause life-threatening situation and death. There could be the following risks:

  • Raise blood sugar
  • Affect your judgment and willpower
  • Increase triglyceride levels
  • Raise blood pressure
  • Cause flushing, nausea, increased heart rate and slurred speech


If you have pneumonia and consume alcohol, there are several risks as followed:

  • Increased inhaling stomach acid
  • Microbes entering the lungs from the upper throat
  • Fewer bacteria that clear the upper respiratory tract
  • Reduce the immune response in the lungs
  • Increase inflammation of the lungs
  • Increase the risk of infection or body fluids


Alchohol Interaction with Drugs


Medicine Examples: Ativan®, BuSpar®, Herbalpreparations®, Klonopin®, Librium®, Paxil®,Valium®, Xanax®

Possible Consequences:

  • Drowsiness
  • Dizziness
  • Increased risk for overdose
  • Slow or difficult breathing
  • Impaired motor control
  • Unusual behavior
  • Memory problems
  • Liver damage (Herbalpreparations®)
  • Loss of consciousness (Valium®)

Medicine Examples: Adderall®, Concerta®, Dexedrine®, Focalin®, Strattera®, Vyvanse®

Possible Consequences:

  • Dizziness
  • Drowsiness
  • Increased risks of heart problems (Vyvanse® and Dexedrine®)
  • Liver damage (Strattera®)
  • Impaired concentration (Concerta® and Focalin®)
  • Headache (Adderall®)
  • Heart defects or severe heart disease (Adderall®)

Medicine Examples: Advil®, Aleve®, Excedrin®, Motrin®, Tylenol®

Possible Consequences:

  • Stomach upset
  • Bleeding and ulcers
  • Rapid heartbeat
  • Liver damage (Tylenol® and Excedrin®)
  • Gastrointestinal bleeding (Advil®)
  • Shortness of breath (Advil®)

Medicine Examples: Acrodantin®, Flagyl®, Grisactin®, Nizoral®, Nydrazid®, Seromycin®, Tindamax®, Zithromax®

Possible Consequences:

  • Fast heartbeat
  • Sudden changes in blood pressure
  • Stomach pain
  • Vomiting
  • Liver damage (Nizoral® and Nydrazid®)
  • Nausea (Flagyl®)

Medicine Examples: Alavert®, Atarax®, Benadryl®,Clarinex®, Claritin®, Zyrtec®, allergies/colds/flu medicine which contain brompheniramine or chlorpheniramine

Possible Consequences:

  • Drowsiness
  • Dizziness
  • Loss of consciousness
  • Increased risk for overdose
  • Dehydration
  • Memory impairment

Medicine Examples: Darvocet–N®, Demerol®, Fiorinal® (with codeine), Percocel®, Vicodin®

Possible Consequences:

  • Drowsiness
  • Dizziness
  • Slow or difficult breathing
  • Impaired motor control
  • Severe weakness or dizziness (Vicodin®)
  • Feeling light-headed or faint (Vicodin®)
  • Seizures (Vicodin®)

Medicine Examples:Ambien®, Lunesta®, Prosom®, Restoril®, Sominex®, Unisom®, herbal preparations (including chamomile, valerian, lavender)

Possible Consequences:

  • Drowsiness
  • Sleepiness
  • Loss of physical coordination (Ambien®)
  • Somnambulance — sleepwalking (Ambien®)
  • Slow or difficult breathing
  • Impaired motor control
  • Memory problems

Medicine Examples:Abilify®, Anafranil®,Cymbalta®, Desyrel®, Effexor®, Invega®, Lexapro®, Nardil®, Norpramin®, Parnate®, Prozac®, Remeron®, Serzone®, Symbyax®, Seroquel®, Wellbutrin®, Zoloft®

Possible Consequences:

  • Drowsiness
  • Sleepiness
  • Increased feelings of depression or hopelessness
  • Impaired motor control (Seroquel® and Remeron®)
  • Serious heart-related side effects (Nardil® and Parnate®)
  • Suicidal thoughts (Zoloft®)
  • Increasing alcohol effects (Wellbutrin®)
